About

Dementia is often associated with aging and is most commonly seen in older individuals, but it can also affect younger people. People diagnosed with Dimentia tend to memory loss, difficulty with daily tasks, communication problems and disorientation. Dementia can bring challenges to family members of the patient which will require time and resources for them to overcome and manage. A few factors of how dementia can affect family members are - emotional distress, social isolation, physically and mentally exchausted.
